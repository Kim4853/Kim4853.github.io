# Research

I develop **mathematically grounded machine learning methodologies** for computational science. My research aims to transform machine learning from a black-box approximation tool into a reliable computational framework for solving, discovering, and understanding complex physical systems.

By integrating numerical analysis, mathematical structure, and physical principles directly into learning algorithms, I develop AI methods that are not only accurate but also reliable, scalable, and scientifically interpretable. My research spans scientific machine learning, data-driven physics discovery, uncertainty quantification, and digital twins for engineering applications.

---

## 1. Scientific Machine Learning and Computational Science

I develop machine learning and numerical methods for computational science, with a particular emphasis on partial differential equations and scientific computing. My goal is to establish **scientific machine learning as a rigorous computational methodology** that complements traditional numerical simulation while enabling efficient simulation, prediction, and scientific discovery.

A central focus of my research is the integration of mathematical analysis with machine learning. I investigate physically and mathematically consistent learning algorithms by preserving structures arising from governing equations, including hyperbolicity, conservation laws, entropy structure, stability, and boundary conditions.

I also study data-driven discovery of governing equations, constitutive relations, and closure models from simulation and experimental data.

**Research Topics**

- Scientific Machine Learning
- Machine Learning for Partial Differential Equations
- Operator Learning
- Reduced-Order Modeling
- Multi-Fidelity Modeling
- Data-Driven Physics Discovery
- Numerical Analysis
- Structure-Preserving Algorithms

---

## 2. Uncertainty Quantification and Digital Twins

I develop uncertainty-aware computational methods for reliable prediction and decision making under limited or noisy observations. My research combines Bayesian inference, probabilistic modeling, and active learning to quantify predictive uncertainty and efficiently integrate simulation with observational data.

These methodologies provide the computational foundation for **reliable digital twins** and data-driven engineering analysis.

**Research Topics**

- Uncertainty Quantification
- Bayesian Inference
- Active Learning
- Data Assimilation
- Inverse Problems
- Digital Twins

## 3. Collaborative Research in Multiscale Physics and Computational Engineering

I actively collaborate with researchers across computational engineering, applied mathematics, and physical sciences to develop machine learning methodologies for challenging multiscale and multidisciplinary problems. These collaborations provide opportunities to validate and extend fundamental methodologies to complex engineering systems.

### Transcritical and Supercritical Fluid Modeling (*Purdue University*)

In collaboration with the *School of Aeronautics and Astronautics, Purdue University*, I study transcritical and supercritical fluid dynamics. My research includes thermodynamic modeling, transport phenomena, molecular dynamics, phase equilibrium, and machine learning for high-pressure fluid systems.

### Radiation Transport and Plasma Modeling (*University of Notre Dame*)

In collaboration with the *Department of Physics* and the *Department of Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering, University of Notre Dame*, I develop numerical and machine learning methods for radiation transport and plasma modeling. My research includes moment methods, hyperbolic systems, closure modeling, discontinuous Galerkin methods, and data-driven constitutive modeling.

### Molecular Simulation and Transport (*Purdue University*)

In collaboration with the *School of Aeronautics and Astronautics, Purdue University*, I study molecular dynamics and transport phenomena for complex fluid systems. Representative applications include evaporation, vapor–liquid equilibrium, mass transport, and surrogate modeling based on molecular simulations.

### Computational Mechanics and Digital Twins (*Purdue University*)

I develop reduced-order modeling, uncertainty-aware computational methods, and digital twin technologies for computational mechanics. Representative applications include parameterized structural systems, scientific machine learning, and data-driven digital twins for engineering analysis.
